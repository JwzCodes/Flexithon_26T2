FocusRead demo

Click the ☰ button (bottom right) to open the controls — they collapse again when not in use.
Hover over the text, or use the arrow keys: Left/Right move word by word, Up/Down move line by line.
The focused word magnifies in place — the text never shifts.
Reading ruler — "▬" button or Option/Ctrl+R: spotlights the current line, dims everything else; the word magnifier keeps working inside the band.
Bionic reading — "Bio" button or Option/Ctrl+B: bolds the first half of every word, works alone or combined with the focus effect.
OpenDyslexic font — "Dys" button or Option/Ctrl+F.
Background tint — "Tint" button or Option/Ctrl+T: cycles cream, mint, sky, rose, off.
Read aloud — "▶" button or Option/Ctrl+S: speaks from the focused word with the highlight following along; drag the slider to set the words-per-minute. Click any word to start or seek from there.
Word finder — "W?" button or Option/Ctrl+K: describe a word you can't remember and get suggestions; click one to copy and hear it.
On/off — blue button, Option+D (Mac), or Ctrl+D.

Dyslexia is a learning difficulty that primarily affects the skills involved in accurate and fluent word reading and spelling. It is not related to intelligence, and it affects roughly one in ten people. Dense pages of text, like university readings or long encyclopedia articles, can be especially tiring because every line competes for attention at once.

Visual crowding is one of the mechanisms researchers point to: when letters and words are packed closely together, the ones you are not reading interfere with the one you are. Reducing that interference — by spacing text out, or by visually promoting the word being read — can make long reading sessions noticeably less fatiguing.

FocusRead promotes the word under your cursor and its immediate neighbours while gently dimming the rest of the paragraph. Because the magnification uses a transform rather than a font-size change, the surrounding text never reflows or jumps while you read.
